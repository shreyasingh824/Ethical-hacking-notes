Ethical Hacking Notes

Hacking: Art of finding and exploiting security loopholes in a system.

Hacking is of two types:

  1. Ethical - Ethical hacking is authorized and legal security testing aimed at identifying and fixing vulnerabilities to improve systems.
  1. Unethical - Unethical hacking involves unauthorized and illegal activities aimed at exploiting system vulnerabilities for malicious purposes.

Types of hackers :

  1. White hat - security professionals
  1. Black hat - bad guy
  1. Gray hat - partly white and black

Steps for Black Hat Hackers

  1. Information Gathering and Reconnaissance: Collecting data about the target system to identify potential entry points.
  1. Vulnerability Assessment: Analyzing the system for weaknesses that can be exploited.
  1. Penetration Testing and Gaining Access: Attempting to break into the system using identified vulnerabilities.
  1. Escalating Privileges and Maintaining Access: Increasing access levels to control more of the system and ensure ongoing access.
  1. Clearing Tracks: Erasing evidence of the hacking activities to avoid detection.

Steps for White Hat Hackers

  1. Legal Documentation: Obtaining written permission to perform security testing.
  1. Scope Assessment: Defining the boundaries and objectives of the security test.
  1. Information Assessment: Gathering data about the system to understand its architecture and potential vulnerabilities.
  1. Vulnerability Assessment: Identifying and analyzing system weaknesses.
  1. Penetration Testing (POC - Proof of Concepts): Testing the identified vulnerabilities to confirm their exploitability.
  1. Gaining Access: Demonstrating how vulnerabilities can be exploited to gain system access.
  1. Privilege Escalation: Testing if elevated access levels can be achieved within the system.
  1. Report Generation: Documenting findings, vulnerabilities, and recommendations.
  1. Patch Assistance: Helping to fix identified vulnerabilities and implement security measures.
  1. Revalidation: Retesting the system to ensure vulnerabilities have been addressed and fixed.

Proof of Concept (POC) in the context of ethical hacking refers to a demonstration that a particular vulnerability in a system can be exploited. It is used to show that the identified security issue is real and can be used to gain unauthorized access or cause harm. The POC typically includes:

  1. Verification: Confirm that the vulnerability exists and can be exploited.
  1. Demonstration: Showing a controlled exploit to illustrate the potential impact.
  1. Documentation: Providing detailed information about the exploit method and its effects, which can be used to develop patches or mitigation strategies.

Types of Security Testing

Based on the Level of Assistance Provided by the Organization

  1. White Box Testing:

    Also known as clear box or glass box testing, white box testing involves thorough knowledge of the system's internal structure, including source code, architecture, and configurations.

  1. Black Box Testing:

    This method involves no prior knowledge of the system's internal workings. Testers interact with the system as an external attacker would, using only publicly available information.

  1. Gray Box Testing:

    Gray box testing combines elements of both white box and black box testing. Testers have partial knowledge of the internal workings of the system, typically limited to specific areas.

Based on the Level of Security Expert

  1. Internal Testing:
    • Description: Conducted by the organization's own security team or internal auditors. These testers have access to internal documentation, resources, and systems.
    • Advantages: Deep understanding of the system and organizational context, allowing for more targeted and context-aware testing.
    • Use Case: Best for continuous security assessments, compliance checks, and internal audits.
  1. External Testing:
    • Description: Performed by independent security experts or third-party firms. These testers usually have limited or no access to internal resources and approach the system from an outsider's perspective.
    • Advantages: Provides an unbiased evaluation of the system's security posture, uncovering vulnerabilities that may be overlooked by internal teams.
    • Use Case: Ideal for penetration testing, security audits, and regulatory compliance, offering a fresh perspective on potential threats and vulnerabilities.

    Proxy Server

    A proxy in networking is an intermediary server that sits between a client and a destination server. It processes requests from clients seeking resources from other servers.

    Proxy: OWASP ZAP / Burpsuit

    Browser plugin proxy: FOxyProxy / Anonymox

    The primary functions and benefits of a proxy server include:

    1. Anonymity: Hides the client's IP address from the destination server, enhancing privacy and security.
    1. Security: Acts as a barrier to potential threats, filtering malicious content and providing an additional layer of security.
    1. Caching: Stores copies of frequently accessed resources to speed up subsequent requests and reduce bandwidth usage.
    1. Access Control: Restricts or allows access to specific websites or services based on predefined policies.
    1. Content Filtering: Blocks access to inappropriate or harmful content, often used in schools and workplaces.
    1. Load Balancing: Distributes network traffic across multiple servers to improve performance and reliability.

    VPN

    A VPN (Virtual Private Network) is a technology that creates a secure and encrypted connection over a less secure network, such as the internet. It enables users to securely connect to a private network remotely, as if they were directly connected to the private network's local resources.

    Use Cases of VPN:

    1. Remote Access: Employees can securely access company networks and resources from remote locations, ensuring confidentiality and integrity of data.
    1. Privacy and Anonymity: Users can protect their online activities and browsing history from ISPs (Internet Service Providers), government surveillance, and other third parties.
    1. Bypassing Geo-blocks: VPNs enable users to access content and services that may be restricted based on geographical location, such as streaming services and websites.
    1. Enhanced Security: VPNs are used to secure public Wi-Fi connections, protecting against potential threats and unauthorized access to personal data.

    Advantages of VPN:

    • Encryption: VPNs encrypt all traffic between the user's device and the VPN server, ensuring privacy and confidentiality of data transmitted over the internet. Proxies may not always provide encryption, leaving data vulnerable to interception.
    • Security: VPNs provide a higher level of security compared to proxies by creating a secure tunnel through which data travels. This protects against various online threats, including hackers, snoopers, and malicious actors.
    • Privacy: VPNs mask the user's IP address and location, enhancing anonymity and privacy online. Proxies typically do not offer the same level of IP address masking and may leak identifying information.

    Sniper

    • Method: Each payload is placed into each payload position one by one.
    • Payload Sets: Uses a single payload set.
    • Total Requests: Product of the number of positions and the number of payloads.
    • Use Case: Fuzzing request parameters individually for common vulnerabilities.

    Battering Ram

    • Method: The same payload is placed into all defined positions simultaneously.
    • Payload Sets: Uses a single payload set.
    • Total Requests: Equal to the number of payloads.
    • Use Case: When the same input needs to be inserted in multiple places (e.g., username in both a cookie and a body parameter).

    Pitchfork

    • Method: Iterates through different payload sets for each defined position simultaneously.
    • Payload Sets: Uses multiple payload sets, one for each position.
    • Total Requests: Equal to the number of payloads in the smallest payload set.
    • Use Case: When different but related inputs are needed in multiple places (e.g., username and corresponding ID number).

    Cluster Bomb

    • Method: Iterates through different payload sets for each defined position, testing all combinations.
    • Payload Sets: Uses multiple payload sets.
    • Total Requests: Product of the number of payloads in all sets (can be very large).
    • Use Case: When unrelated or unknown inputs are needed in multiple places (e.g., guessing both a username and a password).

    Solution :

    1. Start the Genymotion Emulator:
      • Select the virtual device you created and click "Start".

    1. Configure Proxy Settings in Android:
      • Open the emulator and go to Settings.
      • Navigate to Wi-Fi and long press on the connected network.
      • Select Modify network.
      • Check the Advanced options box.
      • Set Proxy to Manual.
      • Enter the IP address of your computer running Burp Suite (usually 192.168.x.x or 10.0.x.x) in the Proxy hostname field.
      • Enter 8080 (default port for Burp Suite) in the Proxy port field.
      • Save the settings.

    Step 3: Install Burp Suite CA Certificate on the Emulator

    1. Export Burp Suite CA Certificate:
      • Open Burp Suite.
      • Go to Proxy > Options.
      • In the Proxy Listeners section, ensure there's a listener on port 8080.
      • Click on Import / export CA certificate > Certificate in DER format.
      • Save the certificate file (e.g., cacert.der).
    1. Convert and Install the Certificate:
      • Rename cacert.der to cacert.cer.
      • Drag and drop cacert.cer onto the Genymotion emulator window to transfer it.
      • On the emulator, go to Settings > Security > Install from SD card.
      • Find and install cacert.cer.

    Step 4: Configure Burp Suite to Intercept Traffic

    1. Start Burp Suite:
      • Open Burp Suite.
      • Go to Proxy > Intercept and ensure intercept is off (we will enable it later).
      • Go to Options and ensure the listener is on port 8080.
    1. Set Up SSL Pass-Through (Optional but Recommended):
      • In Burp Suite, go to Proxy > Options.
      • In the SSL Pass Through section, add common hostnames (e.g., google.com, facebook.com) to avoid issues with SSL pinning.

    Step 5: Capture Traffic

    1. Enable Intercept:
      • In Burp Suite, go to Proxy > Intercept and turn intercept on.
    1. Use the Emulator:
      • Perform any network activity on the Genymotion emulator (e.g., open a browser and visit a website).
    1. Monitor Traffic in Burp Suite:
      • You should see HTTP/HTTPS traffic being intercepted by Burp Suite.

Checklist in Pen-testing

WEB APPLICATION PENTESTING CHECKLIST

1. Pre-Engagement

  • Define scope and objectives
    • Identify target systems, networks, and applications
    • Determine testing boundaries and exclusions
  • Obtain necessary permissions
    • Get written consent from the organization
    • Ensure legal considerations are addressed
  • Set up communication channels
    • Establish points of contact
    • Define communication protocols for incident reporting

2. Information Gathering (Reconnaissance)

  • Passive Reconnaissance
    • Perform WHOIS lookups
    • Gather domain information
    • Use OSINT tools (e.g., Shodan, Censys)
    • Analyze social media and public records
  • Active Reconnaissance
    • Conduct network scanning (Nmap, Netcat)
    • Perform DNS enumeration
    • Identify open ports and services

3. Vulnerability Assessment

  • Network Vulnerability Scanning
    • Use automated tools (Nessus, OpenVAS)
    • Identify outdated software and unpatched systems
  • Web Application Vulnerability Scanning
    • Use automated tools (Burp Suite, OWASP ZAP)
    • Check for common vulnerabilities (SQL injection, XSS)
  • Manual Verification
    • Validate automated scan results
